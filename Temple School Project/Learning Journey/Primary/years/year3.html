Year 3 Learning Journey

Upper Primary

Year Three

Year Three is the first class in the Upper Primary section and is designed for children between the ages of 7–8 years.

This stage introduces learners to deeper academic engagement, independent thinking, and broader application of classroom learning.

Academic Programme

Broad & Balanced Curriculum

Core Subjects

  • • Numeracy (Mathematics)
  • • Literacy (English)
  • • Science

Additional Subjects

  • • History

  • • ICT

  • • French

The Year 3 curriculum is broad and balanced, with strong emphasis on applying everything learnt in class to the wider world.

Assessments

Our assessment methods include Mental Math Drills, Spelling Drills, Dictation, Projects, Oral Presentations and Formal Tests.

Competitions including Big Writing, Whizz-Kid, Spelling Bee, A* Reader, Modular Tests and End of Term Examinations are conducted termly. At the end of the third term, pupils write the Cambridge Progression Tests.

Assemblies

Each academic year, learners participate in an assembly presentation. The famous Year Three Mixed Flavour Broadway Show featured Annie, Beauty and the Beast, Pocahontas, Lion King, Hard-Knock Life, Sound of Music and The Swanage Tiv Dance.

Field Trips

Field trips are organised every term, allowing pupils to visit relevant places connected to classroom topics and learning objectives.

Parent Involvement

General Parent-Teacher meetings are held at the beginning of every term and during the term whenever required.

Special Facilities

Each class is equipped with textbooks, workbooks, Literacy, Numeracy and Science resources, projectors, and interactive whiteboards.

The Year 3 Difference

The Year 3 Team is made up of purpose-driven, result-oriented, and creatively diverse teachers committed to maintaining best standards.

Strong team spirit among teachers ensures smooth lesson planning and excellent lesson delivery.

Pupils’ enthusiasm for learning combined with strong parental support makes the educational experience unique and impactful.
