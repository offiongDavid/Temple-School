Admissions Policy

These are guidelines to the requirements for securing admission into Temple School.

Age

Children seeking admission to the following classes must have attained the class age by August 31st of the year of admission:

  • Baby Room: 6mths
  • Crèche: 1+ years
  • Preschool 1: 2+ years
  • Preschool 2: 3+ years
  • Reception: 4+ years

Academic Requirements

  • Entrance form must be purchased, completed and submitted along with a copy of the birth certificate and a passport picture before the entrance examination.
  • Pupils seeking admission from Reception to year 12 must take an entrance exam.
  • Reception children are assessed in Literacy and Numeracy.
  • Preschoolers do not take an assessment but we encourage parents to let the child spend a day with us in the intended class to observe and ascertain where they are in their learning journey.
  • The following documents must be submitted along with the registration form:
    • A current passport photograph
    • A copy of the child’s birth certificate
    • The previous term’s report card
    • A sample of exercise books for English, Mathematics and Science from the previous school
  • The benchmark to qualify for consideration for admission is at the discretion of the school.
  • Admission to Temple Secondary is automatic for pupils in Temple’s primary section, provided they pass the entrance examination.
  • Successful candidates and their parents are sometimes required to be interviewed by the Head of School or some senior staff of the school.
  • Parents of students will be notified after the entrance examinations, if interviews are required.
  • After the entrance exam, admitted candidates would be given an offer letter, the school and uniform bills and the medical form.
  • School fees must be fully paid before resumption of the student. School uniforms are available for sale at the school’s office.
  • The medical forms are to be completed and submitted on or before resumption.

Reference

A confidential Report must be completed by the students’ school for students applying for space in Years 5 and up.

Parent’s Contact Information

It is important for the school to be able to contact parents at any time, especially in case of emergencies. The school must have parent’s address (office and home), Phone numbers (home, mobile and business), and e-mail. The school should also be provided with the contact of a guardian in case of emergency. This information is required at the time of registration and whenever changes occur in the contact information of parents or the emergency contact.
